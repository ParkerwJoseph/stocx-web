## Detailed Analysis

### Valuation Methodology: 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity

The 2 Stage Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) model is a nuanced approach to valuing a company, factoring in different growth phases. For Shin Yang Group Berhad, this method involves projecting the company's cash flows in two stages: an initial high-growth period followed by a stable growth phase.

1. **Stage One**: This phase considers the company's current growth trajectory, driven by industry demand and strategic initiatives. Analysts estimate cash flows based on historical performance, adjusted for anticipated market conditions.

2. **Stage Two**: Here, growth stabilizes, reflecting a mature phase of the company's lifecycle. The terminal value, representing the company's value at the end of this stage, is crucial for calculating the overall fair value.

With a fair value estimate of RM1.01 per share, the model suggests Shin Yang Group Berhad is currently undervalued, offering a potential upside for investors.
